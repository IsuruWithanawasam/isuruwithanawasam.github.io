About

I am a photonics researcher with expertise in biophotonics, optical instrumentation, and nanomaterials. My work bridges fundamental physics and medical applications, focusing on developing non-invasive optical diagnostic tools.

Currently pursuing a Master's degree through the Erasmus Mundus LASCALA program, I have studied at leading institutions across Europe—Paris-Saclay University (France), Lund University (Sweden), and University of Szeged (Hungary)—gaining comprehensive training in ultrafast optics, laser physics, and biomedical photonics.

My research experience spans optical coherence tomography (OCT), surface plasmon resonance (SPR) sensing, Raman spectroscopy, and graphene-based nanomaterials. I am particularly interested in applying advanced optical techniques to solve real-world problems in healthcare and environmental monitoring.
